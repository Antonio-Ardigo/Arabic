# Batch 01 — Teach

## What makes this batch Najdi

Najdi is the dialect of central Saudi Arabia (Riyadh and the surrounding region). Three markers show up in this batch:

1. **ق → g**: `gaa'id`, `gidar`, `hagg` (not `qaa'id`, `qadar`, `haqq`).
2. **Najdi lexicon replaces MSA**: `abgha` instead of أريد, `wesh` instead of ماذا, `laish` instead of لماذا, `al-heen` instead of الآن, `ugub` instead of بعد, `shift` (stem `shaaf`) instead of رأيت, `widdi` instead of أريد-أن.
3. **Discourse markers**: `khalaas`, `tayyib`, `yalla`, `akeed`, `yimkin`, `marra` — you'll hear these every minute in a conversation.

## Watch for these inflections

- **tawwuh** — recency particle: `tawwni` (I just), `tawwak` (you m.), `tawwuh` (he), `tawwha` (she).
- **widdi** — wanting: `widdi` (I), `widdak` (you m.), `widduh` (he), `widdha` (she).
- **gaa'id** — progressive agrees with subject: `gaa'id` (m.), `gaa'da` (f.), `gaa'diin` (pl.).
- **hagg** — takes pronoun suffixes: `haggi`, `haggak`, `haggha`.
- **gidar / shift** — past-tense conjugation varies by subject; see cards.

## A natural mini-dialogue using the batch

— شلونك؟ — `shlonak?`
— زين، الحمد لله. وش قاعد تسوي الحين؟ — `zain, al-hamdu lillah. wesh gaa'id tsawwi al-heen?`
— قاعد أشتغل. ودّي أروح البيت عقب. — `gaa'id ashtaghil. widdi aruh al-bait ugub.`
— طيب، أكيد. يالله، نشوفك. — `tayyib, akeed. yalla, nshoofak.`
